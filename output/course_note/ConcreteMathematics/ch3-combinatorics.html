binomial identitiese

\begin{aligned} \forall \alpha\in \mathbb{R}\quad {(1+x)}^{\alpha} & =\sum_{n=0}^\infty \binom{\alpha}{n}x^n=\sum_{n=0}^\infty \dfrac{\alpha^{\underline n}}{n!} x^n \\ \forall n\in \mathbb{N}^+, k\in \mathbb{N}\quad \binom{n}{k} & = \binom{n-1}{k-1}+\binom{n-1}{k} \\ \forall n,k,i\in \mathbb{N}\ k\leq i\leq n\quad \binom{n}{k}\binom{k}{i} & = \binom{n}{i}\binom{n-i}{k-i} \\ \binom{-\alpha}{k}{(-1)}^k & =\dfrac{\prod_{i=0}^{k-1}(-\alpha-i)}{k!}{(-1)}^k =\dfrac{\prod_{i=0}^{k-1}(\alpha+i)}{k!} =\dfrac{{\alpha+k-1}^{\underline k}}{k!} =\binom{\alpha+k-1}{k} \end{aligned}

finite calculus

or calculus of finite difference

formulas

  • discrete derivative: g(x)=\Delta_x f(x)\iff g(x)=f(x+1)-f(x).
    g(x) is called the discrete derivative or difference of f(x).
    \Delta is called the difference operator.

  • Let x^{\underline k}=\prod_{i=0}^{k-1}(x-i),\, x^{\overline k}=\prod_{i=0}^{k-1}(x+i) then \Delta_x x^{\underline k}=k x^{\underline {k-1}}
    Quick notes: \Delta_x c^x = (c-1)c^x,\ \Delta_x c = 0\ \Delta_x \binom{x}{k}=\binom{x}{k-1}

  • \Delta_x\left({af(x)+bg(x)}\right)=a\Delta_x f(x)+b\Delta_x g(x)
    The difference operator is a linear operator.

  • discrete anti-derivative: \Delta_x f(x)=g(x)\iff \sum g(x)\delta x=f(x)+C
    f(x) is called the discrete anti-derivative or anti-difference of g(x)

  • discrete definite integral: \Delta_x f(x)=g(x) \iff \sum_{a}^b g(x)\delta x = \sum_{x=a}^{b-1} g(x)=f(b)-f(a)

  • finite integration by parts: Abel partial summation formula
    \begin{aligned} A_i=\sum_{j=1}^{i}a_j \implies \sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i b_i & = A_n b_n-\sum_{i=1}^{n-1} A_i (b_{i+1}-b_i) \\ \sum u(x)\Delta v(x) \delta x & =u(x)v(x)-\sum v(x+1) \Delta u(x) \delta x \\ u(x)\Delta v(x) & = \Delta \left({u(x)v(x)}\right) - v(x+1)\Delta u(x) \end{aligned}
